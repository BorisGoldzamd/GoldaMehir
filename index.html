Golda Meir, גולדה מאיר

Resilient Israeli Leader.
Golda Meir was an influential Israeli stateswoman who served as the fourth Prime Minister of Israel, known for her strong leadership during challenging times in the nation's history.
  • 1898: Golda Mabovitch (Meir) is born in Kiev, Russian Empire.
  • 1906: The family immigrates to the United States and settles in Milwaukee.
  • 1917: Golda marries Morris Meyerson.
  • 1921: The couple emigrates to Palestine, then under British mandate.
  • 1924: Golda Meir becomes involved in Zionist activities.
  • 1946: Represents Haganah at the London Peace Conference.
  • 1948: Israel declares independence; Golda Meir appointed ambassador to the Soviet Union.
  • 1949: Becomes Minister of Labor and Welfare of Israel.
  • 1956: Participates in the Sinai Campaign during the Suez Crisis.
  • 1969: Golda Meir becomes the fourth Prime Minister of Israel.
  • 1973: Resigns as Prime Minister after the Yom Kippur War.
  • 1978: Passes away in Jerusalem.

Golda Meir's Leadership During the Yom Kippur War

Golda Meir played a pivotal role in the Yom Kippur War (1973), a conflict between Israel and a coalition of Arab states led by Egypt and Syria. As the Prime Minister of Israel at that time, Meir faced a critical moment in the nation's history. Despite initial intelligence failures and surprise attacks on Yom Kippur, the holiest day in Judaism, Golda Meir's leadership and determination were instrumental in mobilizing Israeli forces, securing international support, and ultimately turning the tide of the war in favor of Israel. Her decisions during this challenging period showcased resilience and strategic acumen, making her a central figure in the history of Israel's defense.
